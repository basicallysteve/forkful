I've been cooking at home seriously for about five years. In that time I've tried keeping recipes in:

- Apple Notes (search doesn't work, formatting is terrible)
- Google Docs (fine for writing, not built for cooking)
- Paprika (close, but no nutritional data that I trust)
- A spreadsheet (no comment)

None of them fit the way I actually cook. I wanted something that knew the difference between a pantry item and a recipe ingredient, that tracked nutrition without me entering data twice, and that told me when my milk was about to expire.

So I built EatForkful.

## What it does

**Recipes** — write them properly, with steps and ingredients tied to real nutritional data. Not approximate. Not guessed. Calculated from the food library you control.

**Pantry** — track what you have. Know when it expires. Get a nudge before you waste it.

**Food library** — build it yourself or import from Open Food Facts. Every ingredient, every unit, every conversion — defined once and reused everywhere.

## What's coming

Food logging is next. I want a daily view where I can track what I eat against what I've cooked, and see the numbers without switching apps.

After that — meal planning. The idea is to plan a week, auto-generate a shopping list, and dock the ingredients against the pantry when you get home.
